The Oil Crisis
Iran controls the eastern shore of the Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of all global oil transits daily. Any closure, even partial, triggers immediate supply shocks across Asian, European, and US markets. Brent crude, WTI, OPEC production decisions, Saudi Aramco, tanker routes, and pipeline security are all in IRANWARWATCH's scope.

Strait of Hormuz
The 33km-wide chokepoint between Iran and Oman is the world's most critical maritime oil route. Iran has repeatedly threatened to close it. IRANWARWATCH monitors naval deployments, tanker seizures, US 5th Fleet movements, and Houthi Red Sea attacks that collectively threaten the unimpeded flow of 21 million barrels per day.

Global Cascade
The conflict does not stay regional. Oil price spikes drive inflation globally, destabilise currencies in import-dependent economies, and force central bank responses. Supply chain disruptions, LNG market tightening, European energy security, and emerging market debt stress all trace back to this single theatre. IRANWARWATCH connects those dots.

Beyond the News Feed —
The Impact Dashboard

The IRANWARWATCH Systemic Impact Dashboard goes deeper than headlines. While the news feed aggregates reporting, the dashboard tracks the real-world consequences of the Iran war across seven interconnected domains — from tanker movements in the Strait of Hormuz to IAEA uranium enrichment data and OFAC sanctions designations.

Each panel pulls live data from independent sources: MarineTraffic AIS for vessel positions, TradingView for live oil price charts, Check Point ThreatCloud for the cyberattack map, and the UNHCR Population API for displacement figures. New panels cover the military order of battle, sanctions evasion networks, and active diplomatic negotiation tracks.

Every panel includes live threshold alerts — colour-coded CRITICAL / WARNING / INFO — so you see escalations the moment they cross a threshold, not buried in a feed.
